Book to Film Comparison

Romeo & Juliet to Romeo + Juliet (1996)

The Book: Romeo and Juliet

Romeo and Juliet is a tragedy written by William Shakespeare early in his career about two young star-crossed lovers whose deaths ultimately reconcile their feuding families. It is one of Shakespeare's most performed plays and has inspired countless adaptations. The play explores themes of love, fate, violence, and the conflict between individual desires and social expectations. Key scenes include the famous balcony scene, the secret marriage, the duel with Tybalt, and the heartbreaking final scene in the tomb.

The Movie: Romeo + Juliet (1996)

Baz Luhrmann's 1996 adaptation, Romeo + Juliet, transplants Shakespeare's original dialogue to a modern Verona Beach, a stylized cityscape filled with guns, drugs, and gang warfare. Leonardo DiCaprio and Claire Danes star as the lovers. The film uses fast-paced editing, a rock soundtrack, and bold visuals to make the classic story feel urgent and contemporary. It preserves the play's poetic language while updating the setting, making it accessible to modern audiences. Notable changes include replacing swords with guns (branded "Sword" and "Dagger") and using religious imagery throughout.
