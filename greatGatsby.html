Book to Film Comparison

The Great Gatsby to The Great Gatsby (2013)

The Book: The Great Gatsby

The Great Gatsby, published in 1925, is F. Scott Fitzgerald's masterpiece. Set in the summer of 1922 on Long Island's North Shore, the novel explores themes of decadence, idealism, resistance to change, social upheaval, and excess. Narrated by Nick Carraway, it tells the story of the mysterious millionaire Jay Gatsby and his obsessive love for Daisy Buchanan. The novel is a critique of the American Dream and the Jazz Age. It uses symbolism (the green light, the eyes of Dr. T.J. Eckleburg, the valley of ashes) to explore the emptiness behind wealth and glamour.

The Movie: The Great Gatsby (2013)

Baz Luhrmann's 2013 adaptation stars Leonardo DiCaprio as Gatsby and Carey Mulligan as Daisy. The film uses a modern soundtrack (featuring Jay-Z, Lana Del Rey, and others) alongside lavish 1920s visuals to create a hyper-stylized, immersive world. Luhrmann uses 3D, rapid editing, and contemporary music to make the story resonate with young audiences. While the visual excess matches the novel's critique of hedonism, the film also captures the tragic core of Gatsby's longing. The script stays close to Fitzgerald's prose, using narration directly from the book.
